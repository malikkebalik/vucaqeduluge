Nischelle Turner Childhood

Turner was born on February 11, 1975, in New York City. She was born in the United States of America, Missouri. She is a citizen of the United States of America of African ancestry.

She was reared by her mother, who was a single mom. Nischelle Turner grew up in Columbia, Missouri, near extended family members on her mother’s side. As a result, she has a strong link with her mother and other family members.

She began her academic career at Rock Bridge Elementary. She then enrolled at Jefferson Middle School before transferring to Rock Bridge High School for her senior year.

She attributes her mother and grandma to encouraging her to continue her education despite her lack of formal schooling. Upon graduation, Turner was admitted to the University of Kentucky, seeking a change of pace from her Missouri background.

However, the television star dropped out of school in her junior year and returned to Missouri. She desired to attend the top journalism school in the country, located in her hometown of Columbia, Missouri – the University of Missouri.

Turner initially struggled to adjust to life at Missouri University. Nonetheless, she persisted, and within a short period, she was making acquaintances and pursuing journalism chances. Notably, she joined the G7, a group of like-minded journalism students.

Turner also worked at KOMU-TV, the University of Missouri’s commercial radio station and the only one in the United States an NBC affiliate. She developed her journalistic abilities at the television station with mentors like Stacey Woelfel.

Nischelle Turner Beginning with WEHT

Turner was recruited by WVUE, a FOX8 station headquartered in New Orleans, after serving for one and a half years with WEHT. She would work as an anchor and daily reporter – a step up from her present position.

Turner initially declined the invitation, stating that he did not wish to relocate to the city. However, after agreeing to an interview, she fell in love with New Orleans’s culture. She also praised the station’s newsroom, which she dubs the greatest in the world.

After several years in New Orleans, Turner accepted a position with KTTV FOX 11 in Los Angeles. Her job required her to work as a general assignment reporter, but she also worked in sports broadcasting.

Her most memorable sports duties were as a sideline reporter for FOX’s Sunday NFL broadcast.

Turner joined KNBC, another Los Angeles-based station, as an entertainment correspondent after leaving KTTV. She also worked in sports broadcasting, another love, with significant assignments, including basketball televised coverage.

Nischelle Turner Transfer to CNN

Turner made another change, this time to HLN, a CNN-affiliated network. She accepted a position as an entertainment journalist and co-hosted Showbiz Tonight, a show that covered celebrity news and controversies.

Turner began covering entertainment news on the CNN network shortly after that. Her most prominent jobs include coverage of the Golden Globes, the Academy Awards, the Grammys, and other red-carpet events. She also developed a reputation for breaking celebrity news, particularly after covering Whitney Houston’s death.

Turner’s big break came in 2014 when she was invited to co-host the show Entertainment Tonight. She would take over for another anchor, Raquel Rozanne Diaz, dubbed Rocsi Diaz.

Nischelle Turner’s presence on Entertainment Tonight marked a watershed moment in television history since she was the first black female host. She has been a part of the show since 2014 and has received four Daytime Emmy nominations for her performance.
